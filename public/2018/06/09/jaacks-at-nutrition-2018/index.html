Food, Nutrition and Diabetes: presentation by Lindsay Jaacks at Nutrition 2018

2018/06/09

Focus on Meat, fruits and vegetables, and pesticide.

High prevalence/incidence and predicted increase in diabetes. Diabetes mortality has jumped between 1990 to 2016.

Conceptual framework

How climate change, industrialization, urbanization, and globalization influence food systems, which influences diet and environmental contaminants, leading to diabetes.

US meat consumption is declining, but increasing globally. In China, meat production is increasing by 2 kg per person per year. (And diabetes is associated with small elevation by half serving of meat consumption in Nurses.)

Disparities in animal-source food intake.

Global vegetable intake is low, and fruit intake is even lower.
